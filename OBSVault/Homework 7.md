## Stakeholder Analysis

WorkTimeTracker is a Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) project licensed under AGPL-3.0. As such, there are no commercial clients, investors, or organizational sponsors involved. The single identified stakeholder group is **Contributors** — anyone who contributes to the project. Within this group, Galileo Pellizer, Patrick Peroša, and Amar Ustavdić hold the role of **core maintainers**, with decision-making authority over project direction, merge rights, and releases.

---

### 1. Contributors

**Name:** Contributors (core maintainers: Galileo Pellizer, Patrick Peroša, Amar Ustavdić; any future external contributors)

**Status:** Positive

**Expectations / Interests:**
- The application solves a real, personal need — tracking work hours as students doing part-time gigs
- Contributors expect the codebase to remain clean, maintainable, and welcoming to future participation, and clear communication from the maintainer team
- The AGPL-3.0 license ensures any derivative work remains open and that contributions benefit the whole community

**Possible Conflicts / Benefits:**
- *Benefit:* Being both developers and primary users removes the typical client–developer communication gap; requirements are well understood and changes are pragmatic
- *Benefit:* FOSS nature means no deadline pressure from external parties beyond the academic submission deadline (15 May)
- *Conflict:* As users themselves, maintainers may prioritize personal preferences over general usability, introducing bias in feature prioritization
- *Conflict:* Volunteer-driven development means availability and motivation can fluctuate, risking uneven workload distribution among maintainers
- *Conflict:* External contributors may submit changes that conflict with the maintainers' vision for the project

**Team Response:**
- Work among maintainers is distributed transparently through the shared GitHub repository with a visible commit history
- The open license and public repository actively invite external contributors to participate
- Maintainers are responsible for reviewing and merging pull requests from external contributors in a timely and constructive manner
- Maintainers communicate project direction and expectations clearly (e.g., through repository documentation) so that external contributors can align their work with the project's goals
- Conflicts in contribution scope or direction are resolved by maintainer consensus
