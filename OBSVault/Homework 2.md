## Persona 1 – John Falk (Part-Time Student Worker)

### 1. Name
John Falk

### 2. Function
University student working part-time across multiple jobs who needs reliable and low-effort time tracking.

### 3. Screening Question
Do you work part-time as a student and need to regularly report your working hours to one or more employers?

### 4. Description
John is a 21-year-old university student balancing a full course load alongside two part-time jobs through a student service agency, one as a lifeguard at a local swimming pool and another as a bartender at a busy downtown venue. His schedule is irregular and often exhausting. Some days he goes straight from lectures to work; other nights he finishes a late shift and has early classes the next morning.

At the end of each month, John must report his working hours to his employers. Currently, he relies on spreadsheets and memory to reconstruct his shifts. After long days, he often forgets exact start and end times, overlooks short breaks, and feels unsure whether the reported hours are accurate. The process is tedious, error-prone, and time-consuming, and John suspects he has underreported his own work more than once.

### 5. Problem Scenarios and Alternatives

#### Scenario 1: Starting a Shift
**Problem:**  
John arrives at work and needs to start tracking his shift without wasting time or risking forgetting later.

**Alternatives:**  
- He has to manually track his shift starts and ends (excel, notepad, ...)
- Has to rely on his google maps timeline tracking

**Value proposition:**  
A system, that can easily start tracking time, which can be easily stopped or started at any time.

#### Scenario 2: Monthly Reporting
**Problem:**  
John needs to send accurate hour reports to multiple employers at the end of the month.

**Alternatives:**
- He needs to manually write a report, based on his logged working hours.

**Value proposition:**
A system that can generate a PDF report based on logged hours automatically, filtered and scoped to a timeframe.

## Persona 2 – Joe Watts (Vocal Coach)

### 1. Name
Joe Wattson

### 2. Function
Joe Wattson coaches singers on how to expand their vocal capabilities and become better singers. Clients pay hours in advance and Vocal Coach Watts needs to know when the clients have spent their hours and need to pay for additional ones
### 3. Screening Question
How often do you have to check, how many hours a client has left?

### 4. Description
Joe Wattson is a professional vocal coach who helps singers improve their technique, expand their vocal range, and gain confidence. He works with multiple clients who purchase coaching sessions in prepaid hour packages.

Joe needs to keep track of how many hours each client has used and how many remain. He checks this information frequently when scheduling sessions and making sure clients know when they need to buy additional hours. Since his main focus is coaching rather than administration, he values a simple and reliable way to monitor client hours and payments without extra effort.

### 5. Problem Scenarios and Alternatives

#### Scenario 1: Unclear Remaining Hours
**Problem:**  
Joe is unsure how many prepaid hours a client has left before scheduling the next session, which risks scheduling unpaid time.

**Alternatives:**  
- Joe manually checks notes or spreadsheets.
- Asks the client directly.

**Value proposition:**
A clear, up-to-date overview of remaining client hours allows Joe to schedule confidently and request additional payment at the right time.

#### Scenario 2: Client Hours Report
**Problem:**  
A client asks Joe how many hours he has spent in total and when, (the client wants a full report from Joe).

**Alternatives:**  
Relying on memory, and manual logging.

**Value proposition:**
The system can export clear PDF report based on client.

## Persona 3 – Diana Johnston (Lawyer)

### 1. Name
Diana Johnston

### 2. Function
Lawyer who tracks billable working time per case with high accuracy and accountability.

### 3. Screening Question
Do you need to track professional working hours precisely for client billing or audits?

### 4. Description
Diana is a lawyer handling multiple legal cases at once. Her workday is split between research, client meetings, drafting documents, and administrative tasks. Each minute may be billable, and inaccuracies can lead to disputes, lost trust, or compliance issues.

In the past, Diana relied on end-of-day notes to log her time, often struggling to recall how long specific tasks took. When clients questioned invoices, pulling together accurate supporting data was stressful and time-consuming. Diana needs a structured and dependable way to track time per case and produce detailed reports on demand.

### 5. Problem Scenarios and Alternatives

#### Scenario 1: Tracking Time per Case
**Problem:**  
Diana must ensure time spent on different cases is never mixed.

**Alternatives:**
- Diana has to note down for which client the hours were spent on, this makes creating reports on the hours spent harder. 
- Diana has to keep seperate records for each client.

**Value proposition:**
The system can differentiate between clients on its own.

#### Scenario 3: Billing Review or Dispute
**Problem:**  
A client requests detailed justification for billed hours.


**Alternatives:**
- Diana has to gather the recorded data for that client and manually write a report.
- Diana sends the spreadsheets to a LLM to write a report for that client for her, which may contain inaccuracies.


**Value proposition:**
The system is able to properly categorize hours and generate an automatic report based on those categories.
