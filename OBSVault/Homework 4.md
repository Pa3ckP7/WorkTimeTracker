## Interview 1
**Participant:** Student at UP FAMNIT, 2nd year

### Questions and Answers

**Q1: How do you currently track your work hours?**  
**A:** Notes app

**Q2: What is the most frustrating part of tracking your hours now?**  
**A:** Trying to figure out if they are correct. Is the date correct? The time?

**Q3: How often do you forget to log your start or end time?**  
**A:** Sometimes. About once a week or so

**Q4: Do you work in more than one job, shift type, or client profile?**  
**A:** No

**Q5: Would a start/stop timer be easier than entering hours manually? Why?**  
**A:** That would make it easier, because I would just need to press it at the start and the end. The only problem is if I forget.

**Q6: How important is it to edit mistakes in logged hours?**  
**A:** Very

**Q7: Would it help to separate hours by category, project, or paid/unpaid status?**  
**A:** Yes

**Q8: Do you need to see a history of your worked hours?**  
**A:** Definitely

**Q9: Would a summary of total hours or earnings be useful?**  
**A:** Both would be useful

**Q10: Would exporting a PDF report be useful? In what situation?**  
**A:** In the case of an app yes

# Conclusions

Most participants track hours using simple tools (notes, spreadsheets, notebook). The biggest issues are forgetting to log time, checking if entries are correct, and the time required for manual tracking.

A start/stop timer could help reduce manual work, but some users worry they might forget to stop it. Editing logged hours, viewing history, and seeing summaries of total hours or earnings were considered important.

Based on this, the prototype should focus on simple time tracking, easy editing of entries, clear history, and optional export (e.g., PDF reports).
